Charges

The Tear has 5 charges. A caster may use the tear as a spellcasting focus. When a caster casts a damaging spell through the tear, the tear loses a charge and the spell gains an aditional 1d8 of necrotic damage.

Malice

Any creature wielding the tear may expend 3 charges from the orb as an action to cast Inflict wounds as a second-level spell or they may spend 5 charges to cast inflict wounds as a third-level spell.

Soul Rip

The drawbacks of the tear are similar to those of the orb. The damage die for Soul Rip is increased to 1d8. The Con and Wis saving throws remain the same. If a creature has to perform 2 Con or 2 Wis saving throws in a single long rest, the second of each is done at disadvantage.

Ripping Recharge

After dealing damage to a single target, the wielder of the tear can use a bonus action to attempt to rip part of the soul out of its target. The target takes 2d6. If this kills the target, the tear traps part of the target's soul and regains a charge. The caster suffers the effects as if he cast a spell through the tear (1d6 of damage. counts towards madness and soul rip)
