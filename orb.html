Charges

The Orb has 3 charges. A caster may use the orb as a spellcasting focus. When a caster casts a damaging spell through the orb, the orb loses a charge and the spell gains an aditional 1d6 of necrotic damage.

Malice

Any creature wielding the orb may expend 3 charges from the orb as an action to cast Inflict wounds as a second-level spell.

Soul Rip

Any time a charge from the orb is expended, the wielder suffers some drawbacks. This is because the orb requires part of the wielder's soul to fully utilize it's magic properties. For every charge expended from the orb, roll 1d6. The wielder takes half of the damage (rounded up). The creature also gains "Soul Rip" charges equal to the amount of d6. These go away on a long rest. If a creature accrues 3 or Soul Rip charges, they must make a Constitution saving throw (DC 12) or suffer one level of exhaustion. Any non-evil creature that makes the Soul Rip Constitution saving throw must also make a Wisdom saving throw (DC 12) or have their mind taxed by the inherent negative properties of the HemoCrystal. If the creature fails its saving throw, it gains a short-term madness.
