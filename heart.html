Charges

The Heart has 8 charges. A caster may use the heart as a spellcasting focus. When a caster casts a damaging spell through the heart, the heart loses a charge and the spell gains an aditional 1d10 of necrotic damage.

Malice

Any creature wielding the Heart may expend; 3 charges from the orb as an action to cast Inflict wounds as a second-level spell, 5 charges to cast inflict wounds as a third-level spell, or 7 charges to cast inflict wounds as a fourth-level spell.

Soul Rip

The drawbacks of the heart are similar to those of the tear. The damage die for Soul Rip is increased to 1d10. The Con and Wis saving throws become a little harder (DC 14). If a creature has to perform 2 Con or 2 Wis saving throws in a single long rest, the second of each is done at disadvantage.

Ripping Recharge

After dealing damage to a single target, the wielder of the heart can use a bonus action to attempt to rip part of the soul out of its target. The target takes 2d8. If this kills the target, the heart traps part of the target's soul and regains a charge. The caster suffers the effects as if he cast a spell through the heart (1d8 of damage. counts towards madness and soul rip)

Empowered by Hatred

When using the Heart as a focus, the caster may choose to forgo the extra necrotic damage to empower the spell with either "empowered spell" (1 charge) or "heightened spell" (3 charges). The caster may use the ripping recharge as a bonus action as if the caster dealt extra necrotic damage. The caster also takes damage equal to the number of charges spent.
